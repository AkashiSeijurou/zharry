Vocabulary

Solar Mass, Fusion

Space Journey

Nebula

Gases, Gravity

  • Concentration of Gases, Hydrogen and Helium

  • Area's of high and low density

  • Causing Gravity to suck in surrounding gases

Protostar

Nuclear Fusion, Accretion Disc

  • Nuclear fusion, Hydrogen atoms fuse into a helium atom

  • Released Light and Energy is based on the strength of the fusion

  • Accretion Disc will form individual planets

Main Sequence

Large, Medium and Small Protostars

  • Different sized protostars will lead to very differing lives

  • Divided into small, medium and large

Life of Star

Life Span, Hydrostatic Equilibrium

  • Life span of a star is calculated by

  • L * M3.5, where L is the Luminocity of our sun, and M is the star's solar mass

  • Hydrostatic Equilibrium is where the outward thermal pressure is balanced by the inward gravitational pressure

Red Giant

Too much Fusion, Lack of Equilibrium, Expansion

  • Hydrogen runs out within the star

  • Core compresses

  • Fusion begins in outer layers

  • Compressing the core even more and expanding the star

  • Fusion occures faster than the gravity can hold back

  • Name "Red Giant" comes from the fact that more fussion is happening, and that over a greater surface area, it would be cooler, and emit wavelengths of light in the red region

White Dwarf

Contraction, Cools into Black Dwarf

  • If the solar mass of the star is less than 12, it will slowly consume hydrogen for over 10 billion years

  • During that period they will slowly lose mass and cool down, contracting by gravity

Supernova

Less Pressure and Too Much Gravity, Collapse and Rebound

  • If a star began with over 12 solar mass and completely runs out of hydrogen, it has fused the heaviest elements, no more fusion will occur

  • There will be no more pressure to sustain them against it's own weight

  • The core colapses and the outer layers fall into the core then rebounds outwards

Supernova

Causing an explosion

Neutron Star

Neutron Formation, Huge Density, Magnetic and Gravitational Field

  • If the star began with a mass of about 12 to 15 solar masses

  • After the core collapse, protons and electrons combine to form neutrons

  • This huge density (20km in diameter, 1.5 times the mass of our sun) creates a huge magnetic field and a huge gravitational field

  • 1 Teaspooon = 1 Billion Tonnes

Black Hole

Strong Gravity, Singularity

  • If the star began with a mass of over 15 solar masses

  • Black Holes pull so hard that not even light can escape

  • The gravitational pull is so strong due to so much being sqeezed into such a tiny space

  • The formation of a singularity within the black hole

Cycle Restarts

New Galaxies, New Nebulae

  • After the supernova, the gases congeal to form new gas clouds

  • Usually with the birth of black hole, the surrounding galaxy is also formed
